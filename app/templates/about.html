Karivari was founded in 2026 by four Ugandans. We are based in Entebbe, Uganda and Arnstorf, Germany.

WHY KARIVARI EXISTS

Why we started

Uganda has some of the best safari country in Africa: mountain gorillas, the source of the Nile, ten national parks, more than a thousand bird species. The country is ready for visitors.

What is not ready is the transport layer in between. International tourists book through agencies abroad. Those agencies subcontract to operators in Uganda. Those operators subcontract to car owners. By the time a traveller is in a vehicle on the road, three or four parties have taken a share — and the person who actually owns the car and drives the trip has the smallest one.

We started Karivari to change that.

We want Germans to visit Uganda because the country deserves to be seen. And we want the people who own the vehicles and drive the trips — local Ugandans — to keep the largest share of what the traveller pays. Not the smallest. The largest.

That is the whole point.

WHAT WE STAND FOR

Three things we will not compromise on

person_pin
One person, one trip.

The driver who picks you up is the same person who owns the vehicle. No hand-offs, no subcontracting chains, no surprises about who is actually responsible for your safari.

verified
Verified, not assumed.

Every vehicle is inspected and every owner-driver is checked before they take their first Karivari trip. We would rather list ten verified owner-drivers than a hundred unknown ones.

payments
The local share goes to local people.

Owner-drivers on Karivari keep more than half of every daily rate the tourist pays. Our commission is 18% — below the rideshare apps, and structured so the person doing the work keeps the most.

WHERE THIS GOES

What we are building toward

In our first year, we want to be the default 4x4 transport partner for a small number of serious German agencies and lodges. Not the biggest. The most trusted.

After that, we want to grow the network of verified owner-drivers steadily — and make sure the economics keep working in their favour as we scale. A larger Karivari is only worth building if the local share stays large.

We will be slow about which partners we work with. We will be careful about which owner-drivers we list. The selectivity is the point.
